<p><p>The electroencephalography (EEG) biosensor market is categorized into three main types: dry EEG, semi-dry EEG, and wet EEG. Dry EEG uses electrodes that do not require conductive gel, making them convenient for quick setups. Semi-dry EEG utilizes minimal gel for improved conductivity while reducing preparation time. Wet EEG uses traditional gel electrodes for high-quality signal acquisition, suitable for clinical settings. Additionally, "others" may include innovative systems such as flexible or implantable EEG devices, addressing various applications and user needs.</p></p>
