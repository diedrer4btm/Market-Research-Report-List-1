<p><p>The biological tissue staining liquid market can be categorized into three main types: acidic, alkaline, and neutral staining solutions. Acidic stains, such as eosin, are used to highlight cellular components by targeting basic structures, making them useful for contrasting organs or tissues. Alkaline stains, like hematoxylin, bind to acidic components, providing clear differentiation in tissue morphology. Neutral stains, which maintain a balanced pH, help preserve tissue integrity while providing adequate visualization for various histological applications, ensuring optimal diagnostic outcomes.</p></p>
